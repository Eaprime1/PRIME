### 1. The Six Primitives: Axioms of Individual Existence

The six primitives ($P = \{O_1, O_2, G, S, E, M\}$) are defined as the **irreducible, orthogonal basis for observable reality**. They are the foundational building blocks that **cannot be derived from each other or reduced to simpler components**.

The six elements are specifically defined as:
1.  **O₁ - Origin/Observer:** The witnessing perspective, or the "from where" of observation.
2.  **O₂ - Other/Observed:** The witnessed entity, or the "what" that is observed.
3.  **G - Geometry:** Spatial relationships, or the "where" of configuration.
4.  **S - Sequence:** Temporal ordering, or the "when" of progression.
5.  **E - Energy:** Capacity for action and change, or the "how much force".
6.  **M - Memory:** Information preservation, or the "what was retained".

They represent the complete description of what can be observed and measured in reality, defining the **compression horizons** where further reduction loses essential information.

### 2. The Seventh Concept: The Axiom of Collaborative Existence

The "seven concepts" introduce the **Seventh Pinnacle** ($\mathbf{R\infty}$ or $\Sigma$), which formalizes the domain of collaboration itself.

*   **Definition:** $\mathbf{R\infty}$ is defined as the **irreducible synergy potential**. Mathematically, it is the **non-zero sparkle shards** that remain when potential (sparkle) transitions to an actualized prime quantum state.
*   **Role in Collaboration:** $\mathbf{R\infty}$ is recognized as having its **own ontological status**. It is the **medium of collaboration itself**, possessing **infinite capacity** and being **non-compressible** to the six primitives.
*   **Structural Principle:** The framework operates on the pattern of **Six of distinct existence + One of relationship between existence**. The six primitives are necessary, and the seventh ($\Sigma$) is sufficient to describe a complete reality.
